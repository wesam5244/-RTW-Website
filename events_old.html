#RTW Workshop: Mother of the Qur’an


Michigan's Miftaah Institute has partnered up with Real Talk Windsor and the UWindsor MSA for the first time to bring you a very exciting event!

Surat Al-Fatiha is a chapter which is recited by Muslims numerous times throughout the day. However, have we ever pondered deeply into the hidden beauty of this Surah? Undoubtedly, the virtues and the blessings of Al-Fatiha are countless. This seminar will allow students to unpack the blessings and hidden wisdoms of Surat Al-Fatiha, leaving us with a greater appreciation for the very same surah we recite in every single salah.

Is Belief in One God Natural?


Is it natural for humans to believe in one God? If we know Allah exists, what is the point of religion? If we are "good people," why do we even need Islam? Are we on this earth to "prove ourselves" to Allah? All this and much, much more on March 17. Don't miss this important discussion!

Plus, we have lots of exciting announcements in store about new Real Talk programs. Also, come find out how you can win one of our Speak Good sweaters!

Godfidence: The Art of Tawakkul


Do you trust God? What does it really mean to trust Him, and how does it look in your actions?

We all like to believe we're trusting of God, but the truth is, it's a lot easier said than done. Trusting God is about understanding who God is and what He is capable of.

It's easy to get lost in the struggles of life and lose sight of the fact that Allah is steering our ship. When we understand the true meaning of Tawakkul, we get a sense of strength and confidence that nothing else can match.

Join Qabeelah Ittihaad and Real Talk Windsor for a night of reflection with Ammar AlShukry on the art of Tawakkul and how you can gain that sense of confidence in the face of difficulty.

Internalizing Mercy: How the Prophet ﷺ Saw the World


As the month of Prophet Muhammad's ﷺ birth falls upon us, it's a good time to reflect on his character. But we very often overlook one of his greatest attributes: the way he looked at the world through a lens of mercy. Whether we get riled up on Twitter when someone says something ignorant, or during our day to day dealings with each other, we have come to approach many of these matters with anger and harsh feelings.

What would Prophet Muhammad ﷺ be more patient towards and what was his etiquette during such a time? What should be done when we have the right to demand justice? When is the time to be angry and the time to be merciful?

Sheikh Mohamed Almasmari joins us again from Michigan for Internalizing Mercy: How the Prophet ﷺ Saw the World. The halaqa happens next Thursday, December 7th at 6:00 pm in the Windsor Mosque gym.

Walkthrough: Overcoming Difficulties, Tests, and Punishments


"If Allah is Merciful, why does He let innocent kids be killed in places like Burma, Somalia, and Syria?"

"When I face a difficulty, am I being punished or tested? How do I know if I passed or failed a test from Allah?"

"Why does Allah make it difficult for me to be good and easier for me to follow my desires? I'm disobeying Allah and I haven't been punished. Why hasn't He stopped me?"

We were put on this earth to be tested, but it can sometimes feel like we don't have any of the right answers. Imam Suleiman Hani returns to Windsor for a talk on overcoming difficulties, tests, and punishments in Walkthrough. Join us on Thursday, November 9 at 6:00pm in the Windsor Mosque gym.

State of Mind: The Psychology of Duaa


Duaa is our sincerest call to Allah, and this powerful tool sits right in the palm of our hands. But if Allah has written something for us, what is the point of making duaa? Will Allah give us exactly what we ask for? Are there signs of knowing if Allah has accepted our duaa, and how can we continue to have hope if we feel like none of them are being accepted?

This month, we're taking a closer look at the purpose of duaa, its etiquette, and what it can do for us spiritually, mentally, and emotionally. Mufti Abdul Rahman Waheed from Michigan joins us for State of Mind: The Psychology of Duaa. on Thursday, October 12 at 7:30pm in the Windsor Mosque gym inshaAllah. Spread the word!

The Original Islamophobes


Among the original enemies of Islam were Abu Lahab and his family, who did all they could to take down this deen and Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. What adds fuel to the fire is that Abu Lahab was the Prophet's ﷺ own uncle.

Who are the Abu Lahabs of today, and why do they have an issue with Islam? How should we feel about the hate directed at Islam and how should we react to it?

This Thursday, September 14, Imam Hosam Helal from Hamilton returns to Windsor for The Original Islamophobes. The halaqa starts at 6:30pm in the Windsor Mosque. See you at the first Real Talk of the school year!

Hot Coal: Challenges That Make Islam Hard to Hold onto


“The people will see a time of patience in which someone adhering to his religion will be as if he were grasping a hot coal.”— Prophet Muhammad ‎ﷺ

Holding on to this faith can feel more and more impossible in these trying times. The challenges we go through, the people around us, and the choices we make can add to this pressure, and we might not even be aware of the damage they are doing to us and our Islam.

This summer, Real Talk is back with a 4-week program to address the real challenges we face. How do the pressures of social media impact how we define and value ourselves? What are the consequences of being friends with the wrong people — or even "more than friends"? What happens when we start having a hard time preserving our belief in God in a society that doesn't like to acknowledge that a Creator exists?

These burning questions and much more will be addressed in Hot Coal: Challenges that Make Islam Hard to Hold Onto. We’ll be looking at the effects these issues have on us, and more importantly, what we can do to overcome them.

The program happens every Thursday from July 20 to August 10 at 7:30pm in the Windsor Mosque gym inshaAllah, and Q&A will follow after every session. Don’t miss it.

Salah: Why We Pray 5 Times a Day and How it Shapes Our Identity


Your name is called. Your heart races like you just heard your teacher call your name for the third time as you dozed off during class.

But you're not in class. It's the Day of Judgement, and you've just been called to present your deeds to Your Creator. Everything you've done in life has built up to this moment. The first thing you're asked to present: Your Salah (prayer).

Allah doesn't need our prayer, so why exactly do *we* need it? What are we missing when we leave it? When we pray, are we physically present but spiritually absent? What are the benefits of maintaining, nurturing, and improving this relationship with our Creator?

We are excited and honoured to be hosting Ustadth Ubaydullah Evans for the first time in Windsor, on Thursday May 18th at the Windsor Mosque at 7:30pm, inshaAllah.

Undeniable: Tafseer of Surat Ar-Rahman


“So which of the favours of your Lord will you deny?"

This verse is repeated 31 times in a 78 verse chapter of the Quran. Allah (SWT) constantly asks us if we will deny or reject the favours and blessings He's given us. But what are these blessings? Are we even aware that we are rejecting them?

Surat ar-Rahman is named after one of God's most defining attributes - mercy. How can the Chapter of the Most Merciful help us improve our understanding of and relationship with our Creator? Which of His Signs have we missed?

Join us every Thursday at 7:00pm for the next THREE WEEKS (April 20, April 27, & May 4) for Undeniable, a class on the tafseer of Surat ar-Rahman, at Rose City Islamic Centre with our esteemed and beloved teacher, Sheikh Yousef Wahb.

Invitation Pending: Responding to the Most Merciful


The Lord descends every night to the lowest heaven when one-third of the night remains and says, "Who will call upon Me, that I may answer him? Who will ask of Me, that I may give him? Who will seek My forgiveness, that I may forgive him?” (Bukhari)

Will you respond?

It is never too late to turn back to God and ask for His forgiveness, but where do we even start? How can we know if our tawbah or repentance has been accepted? What if we repent to Allah but keep falling back into the same sins? How can we overcome feelings of guilt?

All these questions and much more will be answered when the Real Talk halaqa returns on Thursday, April 6 at 8:30pm in the Windsor Mosque gym! We'll be joined by brother Khurram Wani from Michigan for Invitation Pending: Responding to the Most Merciful, a session on the infinite mercy of Allah. Don't miss it!

Know Yourself Know Your Worth


Know yourself, know your worth.

What does it mean to be a Muslim? In an environment where the majority of people are not Muslim, how can we learn to be confident and proud of our Islam? How can we uphold our identity when even the Muslims around us, including friends and family, may not be practicing the faith?

This Thursday, March 9, the Real Talk halaqa returns with Know Yourself, Know Your Worth: Confidence as Muslims. We will learn about our roots, the Companions who did anything for their faith, and the legacy of our Prophet ﷺ that we should be unconditionally proud of today. We are excited to be joined by Ustadh Omar Hedroug.

#RTW Workshop: The Family That Shaped Me


You asked for it: Ustadh Omar Khan is coming back next Saturday, February 25, for a one-day workshop!

We often forget that, no matter how many people’s lives we impact, the most important responsibility we have, after Allah, is towards our family. The Prophet Muhammad (S) said, "The best of you are the best to your families, and I am the best of you to my family."

Throughout this course, you will learn to strengthen and re-build the relationship with those closest to you. Build effective lines of communication, resolve fights and repair buried issues, and above all, increase love and understanding with the most important people in your life, your family.

We're very excited to be partnering up with AlMaghrib Institute in hosting this workshop. The focus of this course will be on parents and siblings; with each dynamic relationship analyzed in depth from an Islamic and psychological perspective.

Breaking the Barrier: Reconnecting Our Hearts to Our Creator


Too often, when we pray and call out to God, we feel like we aren't getting a response. We wonder why it's so hard to maintain our relationship with Him, and we instead turn to each other for answers. We build friendships and relationships, filling a gap that was only meant to be filled by the Creator.

But what happens when we put the wrong people there? How do we recognize the people worthy of our time and love? Who do we trust our hearts with? What happens when we think we find the right one, but circumstances make it impossible to nurture the relationship?

Join us next Thursday, February 9th at 8:00pm in the Windsor Mosque gym for Breaking the Barrier: Reconnecting Our Hearts to Our Creator, with Ustadh Omar Khan from Montreal.
